Oxygen

Symbol: O

Molecular Formula: O2

Atomic number: 8

Electro negativity: 3.5

Atomic mass: 15.999 amu

Discovered by: Henry Cavendish

Oxygen is the most abundant element in the earth crust. In the free state, it occurs as diatomic molecule (o2) so called dioxygen, in the atmosphere of air. It constitutes about 20.9% by volume or 23% by weight in air. In combined state, it occurs as metal ores in the form of oxides and make up to 46.6% of earth crust.

Oxides

Oxides are binary compound of oxygen with elements having lower electronegativity than that of oxygen (except noble gases and metals). It's examples are So2 , Co2 , No2 , Sio2 , zno , Na2o , Cao etc.

Compounds such as Na2Co3, Naoh etc. are not oxide although they are compounds of oxygen because they aren't binary compound of oxygen.
Similarly, binary compound of oxygen such as OF2 is not oxide bacause fluorine has greater electronegativity than that of oxygen.

Structure of ozone

Ozone has resonance hybrid structure of its two resonating structure. Resonance hybrid structure of ozone has bond angle 116.7° and bond length 1.278 A which is intermediate between (O - O) single bond length 1.42 A and (Oxygen - Oxygen) double bond length 1.21 A.

Heavy water

Heavy water was discovered by an American Chemist called Urey in 1932. He found that 6000 parts of Ordinary water contains one part of heavy water. Water molecule which consist of deuterium isotopes instats of protium is known as heavy water. It's chemical formula is D20 and called deuterium oxide. It is prepared by prolonged electrolysis of water using nickel Electrode.

Uses of heavy water
  1. It is used as cooling agent in nuclear power plant.
  2. It is used as moderators to slow down the speed of nuclear reactions.

Water as universal solvent

Water is considered as universal solvent as it dissolves much greater number of ionic substance than any other liquid. It has a high dielectric constant of 82. The dissolution of ionic compound takes place because of in-dipole interactions. Dissolution of molecular compounds such as alcohols, sugar, urea etc. in water takes place because of tendency of this substances to form hydrogen bonding with water molecule.
